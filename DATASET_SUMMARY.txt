PHYSICAL SIMULATION FEATURES
================================================================================

1. SMOOTH BACKGROUND
   - Generated using Gaussian-blurred low-frequency noise
   - Intensity range: 100-180
   - Natural texture variation
   - No high-frequency artifacts or excessive noise

2. REALISTIC BULLET HOLE
   - Dark central region (intensity 10-50)
   - Simulates cool/damaged material
   - Smooth radial gradient from center outward
   - Gradually brighter from hole to edge

3. THERMAL BOUNDARY PHYSICS
   - High-intensity edge ring (intensity 180-220)
   - Simulates thermal signature at impact boundary
   - Creates strong visual detection cues
   - Provides sharp contrast for edge detection

4. MATERIAL DEFORMATION
   - Slightly irregular circular shape
   - Harmonic perturbations to simulate warping
   - Circularity range: 0.6-0.8 (realistic irregularity)
   - Not perfectly circular (matches real bullet holes)

5. FRACTURE PATTERN SIMULATION
   - 1-6 radial cracks per hole (random count)
   - Low-intensity fracture lines (~80)
   - Random angles and variable lengths
   - Simulates material crack propagation
   - Length: 2-3.5x hole radius

6. REALISTIC NOISE MODEL
   - Gaussian noise (?=2) for sensor noise
   - Optional sensor banding (7% probability)
   - Noise does NOT dominate the signal
   - SNR (Signal-to-Noise Ratio): Excellent
