ESCALATION LOGIC:

Old: Fixed thresholds
New: Margin-based + configurable

Margin = |confidence - 0.5|

High margin (0.3+):
  - Very confident
  - Don't escalate
  - Use this tier's decision

Medium margin (0.1-0.3):
  - Somewhat confident
  - May escalate (configurable)
  - Consider next tier

Low margin (<0.1):
  - Uncertain
  - Escalate to next tier
  - Need more evidence

CONFIDENCE PROPAGATION:

From: Simple replacement
  Tier 1 conf: 0.75 ? Ignored
  Tier 2 conf: 0.70 ? Used (loss of info)

To: Bayesian combination
  Tier 1 conf: 0.75 (margin 0.25)
  Tier 2 conf: 0.70 (margin 0.20)
  Weighted:    0.73 (balanced, uses both)

Benefits:
  - No information loss
  - Weights by confidence
  - Better final decisions

TIER 3 CONFIDENCE:

From: Forced values
  Accept ? 0.95 (unrealistic)
  Reject ? 0.10 (unrealistic)

To: Vote-based
  0 votes out of 3 ? 0.0 (reject unanimous)
  1 vote ? 0.33 (reject, 2-1 against)
  2 votes ? 0.67 (accept, 2-1 for)
  3 votes ? 1.0 (accept unanimous)

Benefit: Realistic confidence reflects vote decisiveness
